Campaign Reporting Dashboard

At Upsight, I design product UI features from spec to code complete. I also lead user testing to validate usability. For the campaign reporting dashboard, the desired outcome was to design a custom dashboard that a customer can add their own custom charts to track their campaign metrics. Another layer to the custom dashboard was to easily compare campaigns to eachother to better visualize which campaigns did better. For the design process, I compiled a few charts together with Campaign metrics, designed the graphs and how the chart looks, created a summary breakdown of the metrics on top for a quick skim of the overall data. Added a top bar to be able to easily move from dashboard to dashboard very effortlessly as well as change the dates, as well as, an app selector to view the data per app. The top bar was also the best place to add a new metric. The green button is a clear way to the customer to see that they can easily create a new chart just by pressing the new custom metric button. I then designed the side panel as an easy way to switch on and off the different running campaigns to test them against eachother. The dashboard name on the dropdown was also very easily edited right from the dropdown. This allowed the customer to do everything on that page without having to go to a different screen to make edits. The aspects that were measured in the usability tests were: 1. How easily can the customer get to the page, 2. How effortlessly the customer was able to create new charts, 3. How clear was the atttributes panel on the right? 4. Was the customer able to name, remame and edit a dashboard? and 5. Did the customer know that they can share a dashboard with other members of their organization. I learned that customers had a hard time distinguishing between the chart colors and that results of customers creating a dashboard was so much easier than before because all the edits were done on the same page and the customer didn't have to look where to go to make edits and to share the dashboard. Customers really liked renaming the dashboard title in the dashboard.

Upsight Dashboard Exploration

We rebranded and redesigned the new product from scratch. Before we started on the actual features and components of the dashboard, we did an exploratory excercise to make a desicion on how we want our product to look and feel and act. The steps I took are competitive analysis, content strategy, and exploratory visual design. The CEO told us that he wanted a clean, modern, super easy to use dashboard, and not to use blue or purple as our accent colors. We chose orange because it was friendly and through competitive analysis we realized that orange is not commonly used with our competitors and that it would distinguish us from other analytics companies. We chose the color and then I starting thinking about the navigation and content strategy. The goal was to design a navigation that would be flexible, scalable, allow for easy switching between pages, and not take up too much space because we wanted the metrics to be the loudest on the page. Then I started designing a few examples that fit those requirements and would look good with the new orange we picked, Pantone 171 C.

Upsight Website

At Upsight, I also designed UI and visuals for the product marketing website, www.upsight.com, I designed and coded the product website, updated the site with newly released features, and designing product whitepapers and other marketing print materials.

Here's a design for the homepage. My goal for this homepage hero image was to show the customer that every business is different. This image is of a mock mobile app. I designed the app, it's a generic login screen for something that looks like it might be a travel app or a food app. The background and the object in the foreground goes along with the story. Physical objects give a sense of a reality. I wanted customers to relate to the images. We wanted to demonstrate that every business is different and analytics/marketing needs are unique as well. The other website demonstrated here is of the AB Testing feature we released in April. Following the real life examples, I designed a bunch of mock marketing campaigns and push messages a company would use to AB test.
